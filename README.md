# Product_Sales_Analysis

Es un dataset que reúne datos de chaquetas Zara, incluyendo ubicación en tienda, promoción, temporada, material, origen, precio y ventas, para analizar cómo estas características influyen en el rendimiento comercial y la relación entre atributos del producto y resultados de ventas.
# Dataset Features

- Product Position (Aisle / End-cap)
- Promotion (Yes / No)
- Seasonal
- Sales Volume
- Price
- Material
- Product Type (terms)
- Section (Men/Women)

# Methods Used

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Correlation Analysis
- Revenue Analysis
- Price Sensitivity Analysis

# Correlation between Price and Sales Volume for each Term:
Each terms has a similar negative correlation. That means there is not a particular term which is sensitive to the prices.  Price sensitivity appears consistent across product types, with correlations ranging from -0.32 to -0.37. This suggests that pricing impacts demand similarly across categories such as jackets, shoes, sweaters, jeans, and t-shirts.

# Revenue Analysis

Jackets generate the highest total revenue, likely due to their higher price point combined with strong sales volume.
Promotions significantly increase sales volume but do not increase total revenue, suggesting that discounts may reduce revenue per unit.
High-priced products show lower sales volume and do not compensate with higher revenue, indicating that demand is concentrated in mid-priced products.

# Insights

Promotional products show 61.9% higher average sales volume, suggesting a strong positive association between promotions and demand.
End-cap placement may not justify additional merchandising cost based on current data.
Jackets generate the highest revenue among product types.
Promotions increase sales volume but do not increase total revenue.
High-priced products neither dominate sales volume nor revenue.
Revenue appears to be driven primarily by mid-priced products with strong demand.
